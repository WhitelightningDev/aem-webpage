AEM Primary Proviso Co-operative Medical Provision Plan

Welcome to AEM's Medical Investment Initiative. This page provides insights into AEM's strategic expansion into the medical aid sector, offering valuable opportunities for investors.

Objective

To establish a member-driven medical provision fund within the existing AEM Primary Proviso Co-operative. This fund will provide financial assistance to members for medical costs, utilizing a pooled contribution system. This initiative is not an insurance or medical aid scheme but rather a cooperative funding structure where members support each other within available reserves.

There are no guarantees as assistance depends on the funds built up in the pools. In the early stages, assistance will be proportional to members' contributions, ensuring sustainability and fairness.

Fund Structure

Member Account

Members can make withdrawals or take loans against their equity. This can be used for doctors’ visits and smaller expenses. You are in control of what you use.

Example Grant Calculation

The Board will define grant calculation criteria but may typically consider the following:

Example Scenario:
A member contributes R1,000 per month with R15,000 in reserves, while the Co-op Reserve Pool holds R1,000,000. Previous grants received: R40,000.

  • Maximum Grant = 4 × reserve value = R60,000
  • OR 24 × monthly contribution = R24,000
  • OR 10% of total Reserve Pool = R100,000
  • Previous grants of R40,000 reduce the available grant.

Eligibility and Grant Calculation

  • 1. Individual reserve pool contributions to date.
  • 2. Monthly contributions and contribution period of the member.
  • 3. Total reserve pool balance.
  • 4. Previous grants received by the member.

Ideal Member Profile

The ideal participant in this fund is a member who maintains a healthy lifestyle, actively avoids preventable illnesses, and is not a frequent visitor to doctors and hospitals. Members who rarely claim from medical funds, prefer alternative healing methods, and utilize natural remedies are best suited for this cooperative model. This ensures that contributions accumulate over time, allowing the fund to remain sustainable while assisting those in genuine need. Additionally, members should understand the risks involved and be willing to contribute towards building long-term reserves.

Financial Projections

  • Existing Reserve Pool: R300,000.
  • Initial Member Base: 11 members.
  • Projected Monthly Contributions: R40,000 (average R4,545 per member).
  • Expected Growth: 1 new member per month contributing an average of R4,545.

Two scenarios are considered, now incorporating a 10% annual growth rate on accumulated funds.

Scenario 1: No Grants Paid Out
  • Pool Growth: With 90% of contributions allocated to reserves, and one new member joining per month, the reserve pool will grow significantly over 10 years, with an additional 10% annual growth
  • Account Growth: 10% of contributions are allocated here, growing at the same rate.

Key Takeaways

Without grants, the Reserve Pool grows significantly due to consistent contributions and a 10% annual growth factor. With 50% annual grant distribution, the Reserve Pool still grows but at a slower rate while maintaining sustainability. The Member Account also grows steadily, providing financial liquidity for members.

Management

The Board are elected by and from the members. A Management team by the members will be appointed to consider every application for assistance.

Administration & Fees

AEM is administered inhouse using an online administration system designed for pooling in co-ops. Members apply online and statements are available 24/7 online. Fees on contributions between 1% and 10% based on a sliding scale. Monthly admin fees amount to 0.083% per month based on the value of the pools.
